Education

The Nielsen Norman Group (NN/g) UX Certification is a professional credential designed for individuals seeking to enhance their expertise in user experience (UX) design. It is awarded upon successful completion of a series of intensive, evidence-based courses focused on fundamental and advanced UX principles, methodologies, and best practices. The certification program emphasizes practical skills in areas like usability testing, interaction design, information architecture, and user research, ensuring that participants gain both theoretical knowledge and hands-on experience.

The Appian Developer Course provides in-depth training on building applications within the Appian platform. Topics covered include application components and data flow, application lifecycle management, dynamic interface configuration, and application security. Additional focus areas include displaying and updating data through records, configuring grids and reports, and automating process workflows, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of Appian development.

How To Design for Accessibility: for UX Designers (WCAG 2.2) is a Udemy course that addresses the challenges UX and Product Designers face when designing accessible digital products by breaking down complex accessibility guidelines into practical, actionable insights. Topics include ensuring link text clearly conveys purpose, using more than color to communicate information, and balancing accessibility with aesthetic design. The course provides clarity on implementing accessibility principles effectively while maintaining a visually appealing user experience.

The internationally recognized Industrial Design Program at Virginia Tech, fully accredited by the National Association of Schools of Art and Design, prepares individuals to enter this dynamic field through a rigorous curriculum and interaction with an experienced, dedicated faculty. As described by the Industrial Designers Society of America, "Industrial Design (ID) is the professional service of creating and developing concepts and specifications that optimize the function, value and appearance of products and systems for the mutual benefit of both user and manufacturer.
